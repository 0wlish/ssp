Birdwatching

Every Wednesday, my two friends and I drove to a destination in the Boston area to go birdwatching. I love birdwatching, and I wanted to introduce them to the hobby. During SSP, we got to experience the tail end of waterfowl migration, peak raptor migration, and peak warbler migration. We also saw a variety of Massachusett's year-round residents. Our goal was to see as many species as possible, so we visited a diverse array of habitats, ranging from shorelines and ponds to forests and grasslands.

We saw a total of 76 species! According to the Massachusetts Avian Records Committee (MARC), there are 516 species of birds that have been documented in Massachusetts. Around 300 of those are commonly seen in the state. Our most common species was the Song Sparrow, which we encountered at every location we birded at.
