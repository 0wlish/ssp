Ceramics

I have never taken a cermaics class at BB&N, but last year my free block lined up with my friends' ceramics class, so I joined their class during my long block free on Fridays. During that time I experimented with creating small animal sculptures, and I wanted to continue that project during my SSP.

I finished eight pieces during SSP, but not all of them are out of the kiln yet. I'll show my process on one piece, then show all the pieces at the end.

Sculpting

Here I form the basic shape out of wet clay, then wait for it to become leather-hard. Once it's dry enough to work with, I begin sculpting.

Bisquing

After the piece is bone dry, it goes into the kiln for a bisque firing. It's very fragile while bone dry, but after the bisque it becomes a lot more durable. The first image is before firing, the second image is after firing.

Glazing

Glazing gives a ceramic piece a durable sheen. It also looks cool. The first image shows the figurine with glaze painted on, before firing. The second shows it after coming out of the kiln.
